Financial Instability: A Cause of Concern for A Country?

- By Armaan Sidhu
November 14,2021

Usually, the thought of financial instability finds itself confined to some tragedy in someone’s Finance or entrepreneurship, as we living in India and living a comfortable life seldom witnessed any time period of National financial instability in recent days. However, this doesn’t mean that they don’t exist, our Southern neighbour - Sri Lanks is facing its one of the worst financial instability period nowadays, and is one of the countries which are facing it right now - others being Myanmar, Pakistan, Lebanon, etc. In this article, we take up case studies of some recent financial instabilities and draw meaningful conclusions from them.

2020 Beirut Port Blast: A Contributing Factor to Lebanon’s Financial Crisis Lebanon’s Economic Crisis :
Lebanon, a country located north of India’s most trusted and dependable all-time ally - Israel is facing the biggest financial instability by any country in recent times, for which the World Bank has stated that is sinking to its worst-ever faced crisis amidst frequent grants, and aids from International organizations.
While Lebanon’s political model and demographics’ ratio make it destined for it as a country to fail, it was not expected for it to be in an economic crisis instead of a civil war. In Lebanon, the top democratic government posts like President, Prime Minister are individually reserved for each one of its religious community, and the religious population of orthodox Christians, Sunni Muslims and Shia Muslims - nearly 30 % each, this makes it difficult to make progressive governments as each religious population votes for a candidate on its reserved post which will benefit the religious community rather than the country as a whole. Thus Lebanon’s politicians care more about their community rather than the country as a whole.
While multiple reasons are cited for Lebanon to fail some of them include providing a safe haven to Iran funded Shia Islamic Terrorist Organization Hezbollah which is waging a proxy war against Israel and as we know, any country which protects terrorists will fail sooner or later. As the country remains divided, people focus more on their religion rather than exports and add to it the unfortunate 2020 Beirut port Blast made the country’s approval ratings go down the dustbin and hence Foreign Investors started pulling out due to loss of trust.

Sri Lankan Economic Crisis :
Sri Lanka - a country sharing a maritime border with India, people find it hard to digest the fact that Sri Lanka is facing a tough Economic Crisis in recent times, but as it’s said that truth is what it is - so be it.
Recently Sri Lanka’s Ruling party changed from a Pro-China party to a somewhat neutral or I should say Pro-India party. The previous government made sure that Sri Lanka gets reduced to a mere Chinese Colony by taking steps such as - establishing Colombo Port City and giving away all megaprojects to Chinese companies rather than Sri Lankan, the country was becoming more dependant than ever, and due to Coronavirus Outbreak, the Sri Lankan tourism industry - which contributes 10% to the GDP and a major part to forex, got immediately shutdown. As a result, forex declined from 7.5 Billion Dollars to a mere less than 2 Billion Dollars by October 2021 and the country imposed Food Emergency and deployed Army to ensure no hoarding of food items takes place as it ran out of forex to import food. Moreover, Sri Lanka requested India to provide a 500 Million Dollar loan so that they can import Petroleum and Energy products.
In conclusion, in the case of Sri Lanka, the reasons for financial instability are increasing dependence on China and lack of diversification due to which the country’s sole forex provider remained tourism which shut due to the Coronavirus outbreak.
Conclusion :
Worth Learning from such incidents is that when terrorists are harboured in a country, politicians care for their vote bank more than the country, foreign dependence reaches an all-time high, and a lack of diversification of Economic Sectors are ensured, the country will be bound to fail.
