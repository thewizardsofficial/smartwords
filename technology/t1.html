Rise in Cyber Attacks: An Alarming Thing
“Data is the new Oil” – a very appropriate sentence in today’s world. Today the whole world is run by some or other form of data whether it is digital data, analog data, signals, internet or anything. Many big companies invest heavily on data analytics so that they can get, understand and use a customer’s data for improving their sales. But, now data is the newest target of malicious people. They use many techniques to target and extract data of a person from the system. Let’s have a look on how they do so and how can we protect ourselves:
What are Cyber Attacks ?
A Cyber Attack is an unwanted intruding by hackers using a bunch of techniques from their computers against a particular network of computers.
A cyber attack can be sometimes very depressing i.e. how can anyone’s data be leaked, stolen or hacked easily like this.
Why are Cyber Attacks?
Cyber attacks and cyber frauds generally happen for some specific reasons like:
1). Manipulate the computer or person to get money or financial benefit.
2). Get some kind of sensitive or crucial information.
3). To just harass someone by doing harm to their network or PC.
Usually the attacker has some or the other benefits of breaching or disrupting other's networks through their PCs.
Most cyber attacks occur at home networks or PCs of people because they do not have sufficient knowledge about these attacks and hackers take full advantage of these things.
Fact: First Cyber Attack was detected between the area of conflicts of India and Pakistan in the early 1990s.
Types of Cyber Attacks
Daily many new cyber attacks are detected somewhere or the other in the world. So, if we want to defend ourselves from them we need to firstly know about them. Cyber-Attacks are of many types. Let’s explore them one by one:
Malware :
The term malware can be broken like malicious (derived from malice, worms) ware. From there we can identify that it is related to viruses and worms intruding the computer. Malware can harm your computer in the following ways :-
Denied access to important file systems.
Losing full control on our devices.
Some forms of malware are designed to extort money from victims. Perhaps the most notable form of malware is Ransomware – a program designed to encrypt the victim’s files and then ask them to pay a ransom in order to get the decryption key.
2). Phishing :
Phishing in simple meaning means to send a bomb of fraudulent emails to users, disguised as they are coming from a trusted source leading to building trust in the users and a good chance to enable users to leak their information to that malicious email-sending body.
Phishing is the most common form of cyber-attack, because it is easy to carry-out, and surprisingly it is very effective also.
3). SQL Injections :
The SQL injection is an advanced type of cyber attack where the hackers try to intrude the system by injecting a malicious code into the server through SQL which enforces the server to reveal the important information. SQL databases use SQL statements to query the data, and these statements are typically executed via a HTML form on a webpage. If the database permissions have not been set properly, the attacker may be able to exploit the HTML form to execute queries that will create, read, modify or delete the data stored in the database.

Effects and damages from Cyber-Attacks:
1). Cyber Attacks causes financial losses in billions to the world.
2). Sometimes cyber attacks are made on National organizations or governments. Thus sometimes National security and some secret data of government may also be compromised or leaked.
3). When attacks are made on:
a. Hospitals: This can disrupt the machines and oxygen supply and thus can prove to be lethal for people’s lives and can cause deaths in large numbers. During the covid-19 pandemic number of attacks on hospitals have increased drastically.
b. Control Systems: Most of the industries and manufacturing factories use systems that are heavily dependent on computers. So, a hacker can attack these control systems and can change, alter or even stop the whole machinery. Hackers hack these control systems and then demand a large amount of money to pull back their attack.
c. Energy Infrastructure: Many times hackers target companies or organizations that transport or generate energy like electricity, natural gas or crude oil to people.
Now-a-days most of the energy generation and transportation is done using computer systems only so as energy is a basic necessity of humans, the energy companies or govt.
organizations become the target of hackers for ransom.
d. Financial Institutions: Money is constantly being exchanged in these financial institutions which includes many banks, brokerage firms and stock exchange centers, so hackers try to reroute these large amounts of money. If they do it on a large amount then financial industries could collapse and civilians would have to suffer as they are the one who lose the money. Also, these could civil unrest and loss of many jobs due to industry collapse.
e. Telecommunications: Many a times these cyber terrorists don’t do attacks for money, sometimes they just want to harass people or the government. An easy way to do this is just disrupt the communication systems and the internet. They use complex techniques to disrupt the internet so that all information flow comes to halt in a region and this can sometimes lead to huge indirect losses.
How to protect ourselves from these attacks:
We should have proper knowledge about these things.
We should write such types of articles to gain awareness about cyber security.
We should seek knowledge from experts.
We should always keep a backup of data so at least the problem of data loss can be reduced.
We should keep a streak of password i.e. multiple password encryption to protect ourselves from these types of attacks up to some extent.
We should check unknown mails many times and identify whether they are from a trusted source or not. Checking can be done in a way that the sender is not asking for any money or data.
And if there is a suspicious link without a‘s’ in https (secure).
